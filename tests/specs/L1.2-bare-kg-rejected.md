# L1.2 — a bare-kg (absolute mass) statement is rejected

**Test:** `test_L1_bare_kg_rejected`
**Layer:** SHACL well-formedness · **Status:** GREEN (built)

## What it checks
A composition statement whose quantity endpoints use absolute **`unit:KiloGM`**
(kilograms, an absolute mass) instead of a mass fraction is REJECTED by SHACL.

## Checked against
The "per 1 kg of the whole" constraint in the shapes: every quantity endpoint's
`qudt:unit` must be `sh:in (unit:KiloGM-PER-KiloGM unit:GM-PER-KiloGM)`. The test
takes a valid scenario, flips the statement's unit to `unit:KiloGM`, validates,
and asserts a violation message containing "per 1 kg".

## Expected outcome
`conforms == False`, and at least one message mentions "per 1 kg".

## Why it matters
Enforces the foundational invariant agreed in the design: every composition
statement is intensive — amount of part **per 1 kg of the whole** — never an
absolute mass. Bare kg is reserved for `itemMass` (class-level, informational).
