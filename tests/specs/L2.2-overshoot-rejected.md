# L2.2 — overshoot is rejected by SHACL (end-to-end)

**Test:** `test_L2_overshoot_rejected_by_shacl[<scenario>]` (parametrised ×12)
**Layer:** SPARQL rule + SHACL · **Status:** GREEN (built)

## What it checks
The full step: run `check-mass-conservation.rq`, union its `massConserved`
stamps, then run SHACL. A conservation violation ("not conserved") appears
**iff** the oracle says some whole in the scenario overshoots.

## Checked against
`SupplyChain.conservation()` (any whole `overshoot=True`) AND the SHACL shape
`futuram:massConserved ≠ false` on `StatementWholeShape`.

## Expected outcome
- scenario 04 (overshoot) → SHACL FAILS with "not conserved".
- all other scenarios → SHACL has no conservation violation.

## Why it matters
L2.1 checks the rule's stamp; this checks the **rule → SHACL** handoff, i.e. that
a stamped `massConserved=false` actually causes rejection. Together they cover
the verdict pattern end to end.
